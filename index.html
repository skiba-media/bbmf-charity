About Us

Founded in 2015, Building Bridges Mending Fences is a community-based program designed to encourage and support mutually beneficial, long-standing relationships between adult role models and children ages 6 to 17.

Building Bridges Mending Fences is a 501(c) 3 nonprofit that travels across the country training, empowering and encouraging mentors to build positive relationships in their communities with youth, their families and others that last a lifetime. Primary Mission: To provide quality, intentional goal-oriented mentoring and relationship building skills, education and training for mentors to be positive, prepared and empowered to serve at-risk, under-served, underprivileged children, youth and their families in our communities. By providing mentors powerful tools to build positive, caring and nurturing relationships with youth that last a lifetime! Enabling them to “Be Someone Who Matters to Someone Who Matters”

History

“Know from whence you came. If you know whence you came, there are absolutely no limitations to where you can go.” ― James Baldwin

Building Bridges/Mending Fences Mentoring was birthed in the summer of 1983 after completing my first day of work as a summer playground program director at a recreation center with 457 kids at the ripe age of 17. I was home alone in my bedroom and heard those kids calling my name. I knew then what I wanted to do in life. 35 years and thousands of youth and young adults later I want to make a difference in our communities by equipping, empowering and encouraging others to "Be Someone Who Matters to Someone Who Matters" through mentoring and building positive, impactful relationships with youth, young people and their families that last a lifetime with quality, therapeutic and intentional training.

Meet our Founder

Born number 5 of 6 children to a single mom growing up in his household while living in the largest housing project in The State of Arkansas located in Little Rock and faced with crime, drugs, poverty and the fact that nothing good was expected to come out of the projects. Rev. Barnes overcame the odds that were stacked against him to be the first one in his family to go to college. Since 1983, Rev. Barnes has held supervisory and leadership roles at prominent companies and agencies (i.e., YMCA, PARK-Positive Atmosphere Reaches Kids, Job Corps, Bridge Way Hospital, Methodist Behavioral Health Hospital, The Pointe Behavioral Health Services, Centers for Youth and Families, Birch Tree Communities, Catholic Charities of Arkansas-Hurricane Recovery, Pinnacle Pointe Behavioral Health Hospital, The City of Little Rock Parks and Recreation Dept. St. Vincent Hospital in Hot Springs, AR, Little Rock School District, Lake Side School District in Hot Springs, AR., and The College of Aspiring Artists, The State of Arkansas, Ascent Children’s Health Services) Given over 38 years of related experience, excellent capabilities and a strong desire to truly mentor children, youth, young adults and others by helping develop them socially, spiritually, mentally and physically while empowering them to achieve their optimal level of health to become outstanding citizens in their communities and society.

Rev. Barnes currently serves as The Program Coordinator for Life Skills For Youth in Little Rock, AR. and The Founder and CEO of” Building Bridges/Mending Fences Mentoring” a mentoring program and relationship building skills training and consultant ministry that travels across the country training, empowering and encouraging mentors to build positive relationships with youth, their families and others that last a lifetime. Rev. Barnes was recently honored as one of the top five nominees for the first annual Honorable Vicki Shaw Cook Award of Distinction and received mentoring proclamations from the Mayor of Hot Springs and The Governor of Arkansas for mentoring. Rev. Barnes recently headed up Youth Cares in Garland County, an initiative to provide youth ages 14-18 with positive activities and guest speakers during the summer and on the weekends. Rev. Barnes was annually a speaker/presenter for Youth Move Arkansas’s National Convention at ASU in Jonesboro, AR. Rev. Barnes travels locally and nationally speaking, teaching, preaching, mentoring and training on topics such as “The Role of The Church/Community for The Fatherless”, “The Mentoring Affect: When One Child is Lost, We All Lose”, Youth-You’re The Captain of Your Own Ship”, “What to Do If You’re Concerned about Your Children”, “Father Deficiency”, “Get Ready for Battle-For Now and The Future”, “10 Things Fathers Should Say to Themselves and Why It Matters”, etc.
